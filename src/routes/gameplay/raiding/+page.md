title: Raiding

Raiding is how you take loot from other factions, breaking into their base, looting their chests, and stealing everything they've worked for. It's one of the most rewarding parts of Factions, and one of the most punishing if you're on the receiving end.

This page covers how to find bases, when to raid, what to bring, and how to recover if you get raided yourself.

---

# How Raiding Works

You cannot break blocks or open chests inside another faction's claimed territory under normal circumstances. To raid a base you need to either:

- **Overclaim their land** — if their faction's power drops below their claim count, you can run `/f claim` on their chunks and seize their territory, giving you full access to everything inside.
- **Cannon into their base** — use a TNT cannon to blast through their walls from outside their claim, without needing to overclaim

:::info
See the [Power](power) page for a full explanation of how power loss and overclaiming works, and the [Cannoning](cannoning) page for how to build and use TNT cannons.
:::

# Finding a Base to Raid

The hardest part of raiding is finding a target worth hitting. Here are the most reliable methods:

- **`/f map` while exploring** — Walk through the wilderness and run `/f map` frequently. Claimed chunks show up with faction tags — if you see a faction's claim in the middle of nowhere, there's almost certainly a base there. Note the coordinates.

- **`/f show <factionname>`** —
Look up any faction you spot. This shows their power, land count, online members, and whether they're currently raidable (power below land count). If their power is already low, they may be worth targeting immediately.

- - **`/f list`** —
Browse all factions and look for ones with a high land count but low member activity. A large faction that nobody ever logs into is a soft target.

- **Look for unnatural terrain** —
Suspiciously flat ground, a lone hole in a hillside, or a random patch of sand in a forest can all be signs of a hidden base nearby. Go investigate — if it's unclaimed but clearly man-made, someone's hiding something.

- **Buying coordinates** —
Some players sell base coords in chat or Discord. If the price seems fair, offer to pay half upfront and half once you've confirmed the base is there and worth raiding.

> 💡 Always write down coordinates of anything promising you find. You might not be ready to raid immediately, but you'll want to come back.

## Scouting First

Never go straight into a raid blind. Once you've found a base:

1. **Note the faction's name and check `/f show`** — how many members, how much power, are they online?
2. **Circle the base** to understand its layout. Where are the walls? Is there water coverage? How many sides are exposed?
3. **Check for traps** — pressure plates, tripwires, unusual block patterns around entrances
4. **Come back prepared** — don't attempt the raid with whatever you happen to have on you

Scout with as little gear as possible so dying during recon isn't costly.

## When to Raid

### Offline Raiding
Raiding while the enemy faction is offline is the safest and most common approach. There's no one to fight back, call for backup, or log on with better gear mid-raid.

The downside is it's slower and less exciting. You'll need to break through their defenses methodically without the pressure of an active fight.

Best for: well-defended bases where a fair fight would be too costly, or when your faction is small.

### Online Raiding
Raiding while the enemy is online is riskier but faster and more rewarding. You can kill their members to drain their power, accelerating the overclaim. If they panic, they may make mistakes.

The risks: they'll fight back, call allies, and potentially have better gear ready. Make sure your whole faction is online and geared before you commit.

Best for: weaker factions, factions already at low power, or when you have a significant numbers/gear advantage.

> ⚠️ Don't announce a raid in chat or tell anyone outside your faction. It gives them time to log on, move their valuables, and call for help.

## What to Bring

Come prepared. Dying mid-raid drains your own faction's power and potentially loses you your gear.

**Essential:**
- Full diamond armor (enchanted if possible — Protection and Blast Protection are ideal)
- Diamond sword (Sharpness, Fire Aspect)
- Bow and arrows
- Plenty of food (golden carrots or steak)
- TNT and a flint & steel
- Ender pearls (for quick escapes or getting over walls)
- Buckets of water (to neutralize lava traps and protect against TNT)

**Recommended:**
- Fire Resistance potions (in case of lava traps or fire)
- Splash Healing potions
- Strength potions for active PvP
- A spare set of tools for mining through walls quickly

> ⚠️ Don't bring items you can't afford to lose. If you die en route or during the raid, everything in your inventory drops.

## During the Raid

**Get in, loot, get out.** Every minute you spend in an enemy base is a minute for them to log on, get backup, or for something to go wrong.

A few rules of thumb:

- **Don't grief pointlessly.** Blowing up everything with TNT wastes your resources and destroys loot you could've taken. Break what you need to get in, then focus on the chests.
- **Check every room.** Experienced players hide their best stuff behind fake walls, in secondary rooms, or under the floor. Don't assume the first chest room you find is all there is.
- **Watch your inventory.** It fills up fast. Prioritize high-value items — spawners, enchanted gear, rare materials — over bulk resources.
- **Keep an eye on chat.** If their members start logging on mid-raid, be ready to fight or leave quickly.
- **Set a temporary `/sethome` nearby** before entering so you can return quickly if you die or need to make a second trip.

## Recovering From a Raid

Getting raided is rough, but it's not the end. Here's how to bounce back:

**Immediately after:**
- Don't rage. Assess what was taken and what's still intact.
- If your base location is now known, assume it will be hit again. Start planning a move.
- Use ender chests — anything stored there is personal to you and can never be looted by raiders. Get your most valuable items in there first.

**Short term:**
- Rebuild your power by playing and avoiding deaths
- Set up a secondary base at a new location before fully abandoning the old one
- Identify how they got in — was it a cannon? A power drain? An inside job? Patch the weakness before it happens again.

**Mindset:**
Getting raided is part of Factions. Every top faction has been raided at some point. The ones that come back are the ones that treat it as a lesson, not a loss.

---

## Raiding Tips

- The further from spawn your target is, the less competition you'll have getting to them
- A faction with lots of land and low online activity is almost always worth investigating
- If a base has water everywhere, expect TNT to be less effective — water absorbs blast damage
- Offline raids are safer; online raids drain their power faster. The best raids combine both
- Always have an escape route planned before you go in
